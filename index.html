Mission Statement

A Service Coordination Corporation promotes the independent living philosophy. A system of beliefs, concepts, and attitudes that emphasize self direction, control, peer support and community integration for individuals with disabilities. To help provide consumer control and direction by the consumer in identifying, exercising choice of, and managing home care services in accordance with the consumersâ€™ needs and personal preferences.

Waivers

Act 150/Attendant Care Waiver

Program enables individuals with physical disabilities to live in their own home and communities. Participants have ultimate control of their services, including the right to recruit, hire, train, supervise, pay and if necessary, terminate the employment of their attendants.

Eligibility Criteria

US citizen or permanent resident; PA resident aged 18-59 with provisions to transition at age 60 to comparable program aimlessly; Physical impairment expected to last for at least a continuous 12 months or that may result in death; Mentally alert and able to mange/direct own care but assistance required to complete functions of daily living, self-care and mobility. Waiver: Nursing facility level of care required, income and resources must be within established limits; Asset limit of $8,000; income limit of $2,022; Act 150 Program: May have income or resources too high for MA Eligibility.

Services Available

Community transition (Waiver Only); Personal assistance services; Personal Emergency response system (PERS); Service Coordination.

Aging Waiver

Program provides home and community based services to eligible persons age 60 or older that are clinically eligible for nursing facility care.

Eligibility Criteria

US Permanent Resident; Individuals Age 60 or older; Asset Limit of $6,000-$8,000; Income equal to or less than MA Eligibility level; Individuals must have a level of care for a skilled nursing facility.

Services Available

Adult daily living services; companion services; counseling; environmental modifications; Delivered meals; Home health care; Home support; PERS; Personal assistance services; Personal care; Respite; Specialized medical equipment and supplies; Transition services; Non-medical transportation.

Commcare

Program provides home and community based services for individuals with a medically determined diagnosis of traumatic brain injury (TBI). Commcare prevents the institutionalization of individuals with TBI and helps them to remain as independent as possible.

Eligibility Criteria

US citizen or permanent resident; PA resident age 21 and older with a diagnosis of TBI who are not ventilator dependent and require a special rehabilitation facility (SRF) level of care; Asset limit of $6,000-$8,000 income equal to or less than MA eligibility level; Has a disability likely to continue indefinitely and results in 3 or more substantial functional limitations in major life activities; Maximum bed limit of six in a SRF with exception.

Services Available

Accessibility, adaptations, equipment; technology and medical supplies; Adult daily living; Community integration; Education; Financial management services; Home health; Non-Medical transportation; PERS; Pre-Vocational; Residential habilitation; Respite; Service Coordination; Supported employment; Therapeutic and counseling services.

Independence Waivers

Program provides HCBS for persons with physical disabilities to allow them to live in the community and remain as independent as possible. Also provides services to technology-Dependent people (Required to sustain life or replace vital bodily function and avert immediate threat to life). Participants enrolled prior to 7/1/06 Age in place; New applicants referred to aging waiver.

Eligibility Criteria

US citizen or permanent resident; Age 16-60 with physical disability; Asset limit of $6,000-$8,000; Income equal to or less than MA eligibility level; Disability likely to continue indefinitely and results in functional limitations in 3 or more major life activities: Mobility, communication, self-care, self-direction, independent living, and learning; Cannot have mental retardation or a major mental disorder as a primary diagnosis; Is clinically eligible for a nursing facility.

Service Available

Adult daily living; Assistive technology; Community integration/ Transition; Education; Environmental modifications; Financial management services; Medical equipment; Non-Medical transport; PERS; Personal assistance; Respite; Service Coordination; Supportive employment; therapies; visiting nurses.

Obra Waiver

Home and community-based services to people with developmental physical disabilities to allow them to live in the community and remain as independent as possible. Participants enrolled prior to 7/1/06 age in place; New applicants are referred to aging waiver.

Eligibility Criteria

US Citizen or permanent resident; PA resident age 18-60 that requires an intermediate care facility/ other related conditions (ICF/ORC) level of care; Asset limit of $6,000-$8,000; Income equal to or less than MA eligibility level; Has another related condition/ disability likely to continue indefinitely that occurred before age 22; Has disability likely to continue indefinitely and results in 3 or more substantial functional limitations in major life activities: Mobility, communication, self-care, self-direction, independent living, and learning. Cannot have mental retardation or a major mental disorder as primary diagnosis.

Service Available

Adult day services; Assistive technology; Community integration and transition; Environmental modifications; Medical equipment; Non-medical transport; PERS; Personal assistance; Prevocational and supported employment; Respite; Service Coordination; Therapies; Visiting nurse.
